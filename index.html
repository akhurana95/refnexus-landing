Why editors use RefNexus

  • Speed: Instant shortlist from thousands of potential reviewers.
  • Fit: Topic-aware similarity plus conflict filters.
  • Coverage: Surfaces reliable veterans and promising new reviewers.
  • Control: Tunable weights (career stage, recency, journal policy).

The problem

Reviewer assignment is slow, political, and error-prone. Editors rely on partial memory, scattered spreadsheets, and vague topic tags — leading to delays, misfits, and reviewer burnout.

  • • Searching across thousands of authors is manual and inconsistent.
  • • Topic tags rarely capture real expertise or recency.
  • • The same few reviewers are overused; new talent is invisible.
  • • Auditing decisions later is painful.

Our solution

RefNexus blends dense embeddings with topic signals and co-author graphs to generate ranked reviewer candidates with transparent rationale and policy-aware filters.

Under the hood

  • • Per-author embeddings aggregated to reviewer profiles.
  • • Topic constraints blended with cosine/Jaccard.
  • • Co-author graph and conflict heuristics.
  • • Tunable weights per journal or section editor.

How it works

Step 1

Ingest

Drop reviewer lists or connect OpenAlex/PubMed. We build a clean, deduped index.

Step 2

Submit

Paste a title + abstract (or upload CSV). We parse, normalize, and tag.

Step 3

Rank

Embedding similarity + topic filters + policy weights → ranked shortlist.

Step 4

Assign

One-click export or send to your workflow. Audit trail included.
